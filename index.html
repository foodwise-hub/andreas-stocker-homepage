Bio

Building better systems for the people who run them

I work in accreditation and quality improvement for social and human services organizations — reviewing programs and operational performance against COA standards, building the systems that track progress, and helping teams turn "we should improve this" into an actual, measurable plan. Outside of that, I'm teaching myself to build: games in Godot, browser tools, and small AI-assisted experiments, mostly to see how far curiosity and the right prompts can take someone without a coding background.

The same framework shows up everywhere I work — including in a tool I built to run it.

Background

What I actually do

I'm an accreditation and quality professional working across the social and human services sector, with a practical specialty in COA (Council on Accreditation) standards review and the systems used to track and report on it — Salesforce Lightning for accreditation management, and Power BI reporting built on SharePoint data. Most of what I do comes down to one question: does this program actually do what it says it does, and can we prove it?
